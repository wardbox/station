---
title: The quiet version of a thing
date: 2026-05-20
summary: A short note on resisting the urge to make every element shout.
readtime: 4 min
filed:
  - design
---

Most interfaces are louder than they need to be. Every element wants weight,
every section wants a card, every number wants to be big. The restraint is in
deciding what *not* to emphasize.

Weights stay medium, not black. If something needs to stand out, reach for
scale, space, or the single accent before reaching for bold. The look reads
refined precisely because it refuses to shout.

It is harder than it sounds. The loud version is always the first draft.
